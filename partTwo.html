Part 2



Soon after his father’s death the Kelly family moved to Greta. Ned Kelly would start breaking horses and breed sheep to trade and shear for extra profit. Soon, their Uncle James Kelly would arrive at the large family farm. Despite not being very good at many tasks he proved to be “a very lively fellow to have about the premises”. However it is soon revealed that Uncle James was trying to get on Ellen’s good side as he was attracted to her. Ellen and Ned eventually slapped him out of the house.

The next day they woke up to find their house on fire. Once they got out of the house they found Uncle James drunk and it is soon revealed that he set the house on fire. During a court hearing for sentencing Uncle James for his crimes, he said nothing in his defence, only saying that he will marry Ellen Kelly. He was hanged shortly after.

A couple of days later, the Kelly children woke to see their mother upon a handsome mare and calling that she had secured a new spot of land. The Kelly family would then start clearing the land and fencing the perimeter. Once this was done they could bring in their cows which produced butter for them to sell. With better living conditions and a good location for selling their products, the Kelly family flourished. Ned Kelly was doing the man's work in the family so that his siblings and mother could ride to school on horses instead of walking.

A couple of days later, at night they heard a person wandering around in the dark. It turns out that the person is Harry Power the famous bushranger. It was during that time that three men: Harry Power, Bill Frost and Alex Gunn, would repeatedly come over to the Kelly family’s hut and either bring gifts or help with chores. It seemed like the three men were competing to see who could marry Annie Kelly. It was soon determined that Alex Gunn was to marry Annie Kelly, much to her delight.
