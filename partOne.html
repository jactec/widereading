Part 1



Ned Kelly’s father was a convict from Ireland and was transported to the prisons of Van Diemen’s Land. After serving his sentence he crossed the sea over to Victoria. Being 30 yrs old and not wanting to attract the attention of the police, he settled down in a regional town where he married the 18 year old Ellen Quinn, whose family was constantly harrased by the police.

Ned Kelly’s early life was full of his family members being arrested or questioned. Therefore, he grew up with a deep dislike policemen. One such policeman named Sergeant O’Neil tormented their family by frequently mocking Ned in public every time they met. It was during this period that Ned Kelly killed a beast that had gone onto their land. His father was convicted and falsely confessed to the crime in order to protect Ned. Shortly after, Ned Kelly's mother gace birth to Grace Kelly.

Some days later Ned saved a child from drowning in the Hughes Creek during floods. The child's (influential) father insisted on freeing Ned's father from prison to return the favour. Soon after, against the will of Ellen Kelly, Ned's father walked back into their life again but with alcohol addictions and would soon go on to beat his children. Later, he became sick and fell ill, dying days later. Ned Kelly, 12 years old at that time, saw his father’s dead body upon the floor.
