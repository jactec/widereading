Part 10



Upon returning to Greta, to hideout, Mary Hearn expresses her life and distaste of murder and the battle at Stringybark Creek. She says that she will leave him if he doesn’t stop and threatens to abandon their baby. It is revealed that Ned Kelly has a child and is unsure how to feel. He at the time felt overwhelmed and said that a baby was a burden however he was on the run and felt happy to have something worth living for and to come back to. She then tells him to ‘get out’ to Ned (as she is still considerably angry) so he and the rest of the Kelly gang left after doing reconnaissance of the surrounding area.

Mary Hearn describes traventism in the Irish community as a plague and something to be despised. (Transvestism is the action or practice of dressing in clothes primarily associated with the opposite gender). Ned Kelly also describes this as an extreme offence to have men make absolute fools of themselves just to attract women. He remembers how he witnessed this all those years ago and describes transvestism as a vile thing. During this time, Ned Kelly had some many dreadful mental afflictions. He was conflicted as to hand himself into the police or continue his robberies and murdering for his own benefit but also livelihood.
